Life On Land

Life on Land refers to the ecosystems, plants, animals, and resources found across the Earth’s terrestrial environments. These environments, including forests, grasslands, mountains, and deserts, are essential for maintaining biodiversity, supporting human life, and sustaining the health of our planet. The Sustainable Development Goal 15 (SDG 15), “Life on Land,” focuses on protecting, restoring, and promoting sustainable use of land ecosystems, combating deforestation, halting biodiversity loss, and preventing desertification and land degradation.

Why is Life on Land Important?

Life on Land encompasses all ecosystems on Earth's surface, from forests and mountains to grasslands and deserts. These ecosystems are home to diverse species, including humans, and provide essential resources like food, water, and air. Protecting life on land is crucial for maintaining biodiversity, combating climate change, and ensuring sustainable development. The Sustainable Development Goal 15 (SDG 15) of the United Nations focuses on "Life on Land," aiming to protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. This includes managing forests, combating desertification, halting land degradation, and preventing the loss of biodiversity.
  • Biodiversity: Terrestrial ecosystems support 80% of the world’s plant and animal species, playing a critical role in ecological balance.
  • Resources for Survival: Land provides food, fresh water, wood, medicine, and raw materials essential for human life.
  • Climate Regulation: Forests and other ecosystems absorb carbon dioxide, helping to mitigate climate change and stabilize global temperatures.
  • Cultural and Recreational Value: Natural landscapes offer cultural, spiritual, and recreational benefits, enhancing human well-being.




Key Objectives of Life on Land

  1. Protect Forests:
    • Forests are biodiversity hotspots that store carbon, produce oxygen, and provide habitats for countless species. Sustainable forest management is crucial to prevent deforestation and forest degradation.
  2. Combat Desertification:
    • Desertification reduces fertile land into arid regions, threatening food security. Sustainable land practices help reverse these effects.
    • Preserve Biodiversity:
    • Protecting endangered species and their habitats ensures ecosystem balance and the survival of critical species.
  3. Promote Sustainable Agriculture:
    • Practices like crop rotation, reduced chemical use, and agroforestry support both food security and land health.
  4. Protect Mountains and Watersheds:
    • Mountains play a crucial role in water cycles, supporting ecosystems and human populations.




Why Life on Land Matters

  • Biodiversity: Over 80% of all species live on land, and they contribute to healthy ecosystems that support food production, clean water, and air quality.
  • Human Survival: Terrestrial resources provide essentials like food, water, medicine, and materials for shelter and clothing.
  • Climate Regulation: Forests and other ecosystems act as carbon sinks, mitigating climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.
  • Economic Benefits: Industries like agriculture, forestry, and tourism depend on the health of land ecosystems.




How to Protect Life on Land

  1. Conserve Natural Habitats: Support the creation of protected areas and restore degraded ecosystems.
  2. Plant Trees and Reforest: Reforestation and afforestation can help reverse the effects of deforestation.
  3. Support Sustainable Practices: Choose products certified as sustainably sourced and reduce waste.
  4. Educate and Advocate: Raise awareness about the importance of biodiversity and land conservation.
  5. Combat Wildlife Trafficking: Support laws and organizations working to prevent illegal hunting and trading of animals.







Goals Life on Land

1. Protect and Restore Forests: Forests cover 31% of the planet’s land area, support biodiversity, and regulate climate by storing carbon. Deforestation must be reduced, and degraded forests restored.

2. Combat Desertification: Prevent and reverse desertification by promoting sustainable land use practices, especially in arid and semi-arid regions.

3. Halt Biodiversity Loss: Protect endangered species and their habitats to ensure the survival of ecosystems and the services they provide.

4. Sustainable Land Use: Promote sustainable agriculture and forestry to prevent over-exploitation and ensure food security.

5. Protect Mountain Ecosystems: Mountains are critical for biodiversity, water supply, and climate regulation, requiring special attention to conservation efforts.
